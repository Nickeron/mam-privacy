2. What We Collect and Why

2.1 Google Play Games identity, leaderboard, and achievements

When you sign in with Google Play Games (GPGS), the app receives an anonymous pseudonymous player identifier assigned by Google. This identifier is scoped to your Google Play Games account and is not your name, email address, or any other directly identifying information. The app never reads your Google account name or email.

We use this identifier to submit your score to the in-game leaderboard and to report your progress on Google Play Games achievements. Google acts as a data processor on our behalf for this service. Signing in with Google Play Games is optional — if you decline, you can still play; the leaderboard and achievements features simply are not available. Cloud save does not depend on GPGS sign-in; it is described exclusively in Section 2.2.

2.2 Firebase anonymous identity and Firestore cloud save

On first launch the app creates an anonymous Firebase account, which yields a random pseudonymous user identifier (UID) generated by Google's Firebase Authentication. This UID is not your name, email address, or any other directly identifying information, and is not linked to your Google account. It is stored on your device and persists across launches so the app can recognise the same install.

When an anonymous Firebase account exists, the app stores a copy of your game-progress data in Google's Cloud Firestore database, in a document keyed to your anonymous Firebase UID. Access is restricted so each document can be read or written only by the anonymous account that owns it. Your local on-device save is always authoritative; the cloud copy is an additive backup used to restore progress and sync across devices. Google acts as a data processor on our behalf.

2.3 Game progress stored locally

The following game state is stored on your device (in Android app storage and a private save file): level progress (stars earned, highest level), currency balances (coins, diamonds, stars, cookie-jar balance), hero and monster unlock states, boost upgrade levels, total playtime (a relative counter used for save-conflict resolution), quest and reward-pass state, achievement progress and unlocks, lifetime gameplay statistics, cosmetic items owned and equipped, and one-time purchase entitlement flags. This data is held on your device and, as described in Section 2.2, backed up to Cloud Firestore keyed to your anonymous Firebase identifier. It is not shared with any third party other than Google as the cloud-save processor.

2.4 In-app purchases

When you make a real-money purchase, payment is processed entirely by Google Play Billing — we never receive or store your payment card details, billing address, or any payment-method information.

To confirm a purchase is genuine before granting the item (fraud prevention), the app sends the Google Play purchase token to a Firebase Cloud Function we operate. That function verifies the token with the Google Play Developer API and stores a minimal validation record in Google's Cloud Firestore: the purchase token, your pseudonymous Firebase user ID, the product identifier, and a validation timestamp. This record exists solely to stop a purchase from being granted more than once; it contains no payment or contact information. If you redeem a promotional code, we likewise record that your pseudonymous Firebase user ID claimed that code, to stop the same code being claimed twice. The purchase also generates an analytics event as described in Section 2.5.

2.5 Analytics (consent-gated)

On first launch, the app shows a consent dialog. Analytics collection begins only if you actively consent. If you decline or do not respond, no analytics events are sent. You can withdraw consent at any time from the in-app Settings menu.

If you consent, we collect the following via Firebase Analytics (a Google product):

  • A pseudonymous per-install identifier assigned by the Firebase SDK on installation. It is not linked to your Google account or any other identity and resets on uninstall/reinstall.
  • Gameplay event parameters — level numbers, star counts, attempt counts, boost types used, purchase SKUs and amounts, tutorial step identifiers, and session counts. No parameter contains a name, email, location, or any personally identifying value.
  • Device and OS metadata — device model, Android OS version, and country (country-code granularity only). This is collected automatically by the Firebase SDK.

Purpose: to understand how players engage with the game so we can improve it. Processor: Firebase Analytics is operated by Google LLC; data is transmitted to and stored on Google's servers in the United States under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework. Retention: 60 days (the Firebase default), after which individual event records are deleted.

We do not collect your name, email, phone number, or postal address; precise (GPS) location; advertising identifiers (we use no advertising SDK); camera, microphone, contacts, or calendar data; or any information from your device's files beyond the app's own save data.

2.6 Crash reports (consent-gated)

Crash and stability reporting is provided by Firebase Crashlytics (a Google product). Like analytics, crash collection is consent-gated: it is off by default and is enabled only if you actively consent via the first-launch dialog. You can withdraw consent at any time in Settings.

If you consent, when the app crashes or records a non-fatal error we collect device and OS metadata (device model, manufacturer, Android OS version, app version, and device state such as free memory and orientation), a crash stack trace, and diagnostic keys/logs the app attaches to aid debugging (e.g. the current level number). These contain no name, email, location, or other personally identifying value.

Processor: Firebase Crashlytics is operated by Google LLC; data is stored on Google's servers in the United States under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework. Retention: 90 days (the Firebase default).

6. Your Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have the rights to: access a copy of your personal data; rectification of inaccurate data; erasure (“right to be forgotten”); restriction of processing; objection to processing; withdrawal of consent (for analytics and crash reporting, at any time via in-app Settings — withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal); and data portability.

For erasure: uninstalling the app removes the local copy of your game progress. To delete the Firestore cloud save, your anonymous Firebase account, and the purchase-validation and promo-redemption records, contact us at the address below and we will run our account-deletion routine — the steps are described on our account & data deletion page. For analytics data, we will process a deletion request for your app_instance_id if you can provide it, or by deleting the relevant event stream.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Dutch data protection authority, the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP), at autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl.

To exercise any of these rights, contact piratebeer2019@gmail.com. We will respond to verified requests within 30 days and may need to verify your identity first.

7. Security

The only personal data we hold on servers we operate lives in Google's Cloud Firestore: your game-progress cloud save (Section 2.2) and the minimal purchase-validation and promo-redemption records described in Section 2.4. Firestore security rules restrict your cloud save so it can be read or written only by the anonymous Firebase account that owns it, and deny clients any direct access to the purchase-validation and promo-redemption records — those are written only by authenticated Firebase Cloud Functions we control. Game progress is also stored on your device (under Android's private app storage, inaccessible to other apps). All data is transmitted to Firebase over HTTPS (TLS 1.2+); we do not transmit raw personal data over unencrypted connections.
